This Summer in Shanghai

I went to Shanghai during my summer break with my family. Shanghai is an ever-changing city, where skyscrapers seem to pop up overnight. One of my finest memories of Shanghai was my father wake us up and took us to the Fuzhou Lu old street. We run downstairs, outside, down the concrete steps, and into the city streets, where lights poured out and the lingering smell of food from a nearby street vendor.

This tour in Shanghai makes me have a general impression of this place. It is great to appreciate the classic scenery of China and experience the modern atmosphere at the same time. I really hope that I could have a nother chance to visit to Shanghai again.

This Summer in ZEIT

ZEIT is a Gelato workshop. The big different in this Gelato store is ZEIT contain wine and expresso coffee in Gelato. This summer, I work in ZEIT and it was a great and wonderful experience in there! The people are nice and I can try 10 different kinds of Gelato everyday with alcohol! Gelato is very creamier, smoother and silkier, as well as denser and more elastic and fluid, than American ice cream.

Alcohol and gelato? Absolutely! The variety of spirits, wines, champagnes, ales and beers is vast and unique as are gelato flavors, and combining these two could be a match made in heaven.Cheers! Here is yet another way to enjoy gelato, the beloved Italian treat that Taiwan has come to embrace. Served in a traditional bowl,on a cone or in a wine or spirits glass,

alcohol-infused gelato will keep you and your customers celebrating all year long. Salute!

My 24 Birthday in this Summer!!!!

I’ve been blessed with amazing people around me, who support me, and teach me so much just by them being themselves. I’ve been lucky enough to fall in love with my boyfriend Eric.He helpes me understand what I bring to a relationship. He made me want to be better.

So at 24, it’s become evident that even though I have experienced a lot and learned more, I’ve only just begun. There’s no time to dwell on what could’ve been, what I don’t have, or where that I may not be where I’d like to be professionally. Call it cliché, but everything happens for a reason. The lowest lows can be the best lessons; lessons needed for later use.

I don’t believe in the pursuit of perfection. Perfection is unattainable. Instead, I am working on “perfecting the pursuit”; weaving the knowledge from lessons learned into my everyday life and working on becoming the best man I can be.
